FEATURES OF VERMS

SELF CHECK-IN

Self check-in discard the use of paperworks and save the salary of receptionist. VERMS help you to create a great professional guest experience by welcoming your guests in a smart and digital way, which looks completely professional. It also reduces the chances of mistakes.

If any visitor or guest has visited once, it doesn't need to fill complete form again because VERMS saves data of visitors on database. Visitor just need to enter his contact number or CNIC number to check-in automatically.

QR CODE GENERATE

Scaning with barcode is quick but it doesn't contain multiple information. QR Code carry a lot of information both vertically and horizontally and can be printed or save on the phone for scanning. QR Code is also very quick and easy to scan.

The QR Code feature is specially designed for Events Visitor & Exhibitor. Because there are many vendors who need to get the detail of visitor to contact with them for future contracts. By using this feature both vendors and visitor can get their contact information by scanning QR code.

COOPERATE SMARTLY

You can leave your brand logo on visitor badge which display on front of the visitor badge. VERMS also gives opportunity to client for customizing logo and badge.

VERMS has also visitor photo capturing feature. Which print on the visitor badge. This feature helps you to recognize your visitor perfectly with the help of a badge provided to them on registration.

VERMS also notify the host about visitor check-in. Which saves the time of both guest and host.

PRE REGISTER VISITOR

VERMS also offers Pre Register Visitor feature. Pre Registration activities are those which is done prior to guest arrival at your organization and is also one of the most important stage in guest cycle. Pre Registration is normally done for Groups of VIP / VVIP guests and visitors.

Pre Registration also saves the time of your VIP/ VVIP guest. Which surely make your respect infront of your guest or visitor. And it makes your organization more professional.
